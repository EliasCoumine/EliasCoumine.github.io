In my first year at uOttawa, I took SEG2900 (Professional Communication and Responsibility), where we were tasked with creating a mock startup and building a website to promote it. My team developed a concept called NutriSnap—a mobile app that would analyze the nutritional value of food from a photo. While we didn’t build the app itself, we designed and developed a promotional website using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to showcase our idea. This project introduced me to front-end development and gave me hands-on experience in planning layouts, styling, and working collaboratively.

In terms of my process, I like to start by sketching or outlining the structure of the page on paper, followed by building the layout in HTML, and then progressively styling it with CSS. I often focus on layout clarity, visual hierarchy, and accessibility from the beginning. When working in a team, I try to divide responsibilities clearly and iterate based on group feedback to improve the final design
